Work Mode & Stress Patterns

Across all 5,000 employees, stress levels are distributed across all three work arrangements — remote, hybrid, and onsite. The data shows that high stress is not exclusive to any single work mode. However, patterns emerge when we layer in additional factors like company support, sleep quality, and hours worked.

↑ See Stress Distribution chart above

The Role of Company Support

Employees who report higher company support for remote work tend to show associations with better work-life balance ratings and lower social isolation scores. This pattern is visible across industries and job roles when you filter by company support level using the controls above.

↑ Try filtering by Company Support in the heatmap

Sleep, Isolation & Risk

A subset of employees in this dataset shows a clustering of multiple wellbeing concerns simultaneously: high stress, poor sleep, high social isolation, and low work-life balance. These employees are flagged as "Very High Risk" in the Risk Level analysis. Use the Scatter Plot's Individual mode to explore their distribution.

What the Data Suggests

Methodology & Limitations

This dashboard visualizes a dataset of 5,000 employees across various industries and regions. This dashboard shows patterns and associations in the dataset. It does not prove that remote work causes mental health outcomes. The dataset contains 1,196 employees with unreported mental health conditions and 1,629 with unreported physical activity — these are shown as "Not Reported" throughout. All statistics shown reflect the filtered subset of employees currently selected.
